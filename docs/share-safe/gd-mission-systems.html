Overview

General Dynamics Mission Systems (GDMS) is the C5ISR products-and-integration arm of General Dynamics, formed in January 2015 by combining GD C4 Systems and GD Advanced Information Systems [S2]. It sits with GDIT inside GD's Technologies segment, which booked $13.47B revenue and $1.28B operating earnings (9.5% margin) in FY2025 with roughly 40,000 employees across the two units [S3]. GDMS itself is roughly 12,000 people across 80+ facilities (est.), headquartered in the northern Virginia area [S28]. Its franchise positions are secure communications and crypto (TACLANE, NGLD-M) [S1][S11], Navy submarine electronics and lifecycle support (including the Progeny Systems business) [S21], strategic-deterrence weapon C2 [S27], and space ground segments (MUOS, SDA's Proliferated Warfighter Space Architecture) [S12][S6]. We do not collide with them on hardware. We collide on the services and integration adjacencies wrapped around major programs: SDA/SSC ground operations and sustainment, Navy combat-system engineering and software support, and Army C5ISR integration, where GDMS sells engineering services as pull-through around its own product baselines and its GDIT sibling primes the pure-services work [S7][S21][S15].

Threat Summary

GDMS hurts us where an engineering-services scope sits next to hardware they already own. On MUOS ground sustainment they are the sole-source incumbent with up to $2.2B of runway [S12][S13][S14]; on SDA ground they hold both the operations-and-integration and ground-management franchises [S6][S7]; on submarines Progeny gives them the software and lifecycle-services lane inside NAVSEA programs [S21]. When a customer bundles integration services with the fielded baseline, we start from behind. The compounding threat is the GD family play: GDMS takes the product-adjacent work while GDIT primes the large consolidated services contracts (for example the $988M Navy SACSS C5ISR support award) [S15], squeezing mid-size primes from both sides. They rarely meet us head-on in SETA, T&E, or range support; the fight is on systems-integration and O&M task orders at SSC/SDA, NAVSEA, and Army C5ISR programs.

Their Discriminators

  • Ownership of fielded baselines: the customer's crypto devices, satcom ground system, or combat-system components are often GDMS products, which makes them the default choice for the surrounding engineering and sustainment work [S11][S12][S21].
  • NSA-certified crypto pedigree and classified infrastructure: Type 1 encryption franchise (NGLD-M: 265,000 units planned over 10 years) that few companies can touch [S11].
  • Proliferated-constellation ground expertise at SDA: they designed and operate the Tranche 1 ops centers and won the follow-on ground management and integration design contract [S7][S6].
  • Corporate reach-back and balance sheet of a $52.6B prime [S4], plus a services sibling (GDIT) that lets the GD family bid both product and services sides of a mission area [S3][S15].
  • Seven decades of strategic-deterrence weapon C2 heritage that anchors credibility with nuclear-enterprise customers [S27].

Strengths

  • Incumbent sole-source position on MUOS ground sustainment (10-year IDIQ awarded 2019 at $731.8M; max potential value $2.2B per GD's 10-K; fresh $106M SSC task order with options to $294.9M through May 2031) [S12][S13][S14].
  • Anchor SDA ground franchises: $324.5M Tranche 1 Operations & Integration (2022, teamed with Iridium, ops centers at Grand Forks AFB) and $491.6M Ground Management and Integration design and development (Sept 2024) [S7][S8][S6].
  • Growing again after a flat stretch: Mission Systems revenue up 11.7% in Q1 2026, driven by space and international C5ISR volume; Technologies backlog $17.7B, book-to-bill 1.3x [S5].
  • Deep Navy submarine services bench via Progeny Systems: $121M submarine software development, $66M DARTS, $15.3M security-toolkit award (to $91M with options), and an $18.2M AUKUS weapon-launch/simulation award [S21][S20][S23][S22].
  • Open-architecture positioning on Army C5ISR: won the initial CMFF (CMOSS Mounted Form Factor) prototype award, $28.3M over two years via the C5 Consortium OTA [S9][S10].
  • Stable, connected leadership: president Chris Brady since January 2019, a six-time Wash100 honoree, with recent senior hires in strategy, IT, and space [S17][S18][S19].

Weaknesses

W1 — Product-OEM posture limits objectivity on services work

GDMS is fundamentally a product and platform-integration house: crypto devices, satcom ground systems, combat-system hardware and software baselines [S11][S12][S21]. On any scope that involves independent assessment, test, or government-side advisory work touching those baselines, they carry an organizational-conflict-of-interest burden a non-OEM services prime does not. Their MUOS sustainment position is sole-source around their own system [S12], which customers increasingly flag as lock-in risk when they push for open architectures and data rights.

W2 — Multi-year revenue stagnation during legacy-program transition

GD reported a revenue decline at Mission Systems in 2024, attributing it to the transition "from legacy work to new growth lines of business," and Technologies operating earnings fell 2% that year [S25]. GD's FY2025 10-K notes GDIT grew faster than Mission Systems, diluting segment margin [S3]. The unit only returned to clear growth in Q1 2026 [S5]. Capture teams can expect them to defend legacy revenue aggressively while their growth bets (space, CMFF) are still converting.

W3 — Second priority inside GD Technologies for services captures

The big consolidated services wins in their own back yard go to sibling GDIT, not GDMS: the $988M Navy SACSS C5ISR support contract is a GDIT award [S15], and GD's 10-K describes the two units going to market together "when appropriate" [S3]. On a pure engineering-services evaluation, GDMS's own past performance is product-flavored; they either lean on GDIT CPARS (inviting relevance challenges) or bid work that is not their core.

W4 — History of losing software-defined competitions to more agile entrants

When the Army moved DCGS-A to a commercial-software model, GDMS lost to Raytheon and Palantir (March 2018) and GAO denied its protest of the rejection of its proposal (B-416181) [S16]. Dated, but it is the documented pattern: where an evaluation rewards commercial software agility over MIL-SPEC integration heritage, their model has been beaten.

W5 — Thin footprint in our anchor geographies

Their Huntsville presence is a single Cummings Research Park office, listed in public directories at an estimated 100 to 249 staff [S26], against the thousands of local engineers the Redstone customer community expects from an entrenched services partner. Public materials show no comparable footprint at the USAF test-enterprise centers (needs internal input: confirm any GDMS staffing at Eglin, Edwards, or on AFTC task orders).

Intel & Proof Points

PP1 — SDA Ground Management and Integration award, $491.6M

Space Development Agency awarded GDMS a $491.6M design and development contract for the Ground, Management and Integration (GMI) program, announced September 2024 [S6].

PP2 — SDA Tranche 1 Operations & Integration, $324.5M

SDA awarded the Tranche 1 ground Operations and Integration contract to GDMS at $324,516,613 (May 2022), including developing and operating operations centers at Grand Forks AFB; GDMS teamed with Iridium [S7][S8].

PP3 — Army CMFF prototype award, $28.3M OTA

The Army (PEO C3N, PM Mission Command) awarded GDMS a $28.3M, two-year award through the C5 Consortium OTA to deliver initial CMOSS Mounted Form Factor prototype systems, announced October 6, 2025. Production remains to be competed [S9][S10].

PP4 — NGLD-M crypto key loader, $229M over 10 years

Army awarded GDMS a $229M contract (February 2022) to build the NSA-certified Next Generation Load Device-Medium, with a 10-year period of performance and planned procurement of 265,000 units [S11].

PP5 — MUOS ground sustainment: sole-source incumbency to 2031

Navy awarded GDMS a $731.8M, 10-year sole-source IDIQ for MUOS ground system sustainment (November 2019) [S12]; GD's FY2024 10-K states a maximum potential value of $2.2B for MUOS sustainment under the Space Force [S13]; Space Systems Command issued a $106M sustainment/modernization task order with base-plus-options value of $294.9M running to May 2031 [S14].

PP6 — Segment financials and trajectory

GD Technologies FY2025: revenue $13.47B, operating earnings $1.28B, margin 9.5%, ~40,000 employees; GDIT grew faster than Mission Systems, pressuring segment margin [S3]. FY2025 segment backlog $16.7B with book-to-bill of 1.2x at both units [S4]. Q1 2026: segment revenue $3.6B (+4.2%), Mission Systems +11.7% on space and international C5ISR volume, backlog $17.7B, book-to-bill 1.3x [S5].

PP7 — GD family services capture goes through GDIT

The $988M Navy SACSS award (December 2025) for C5ISR integration, engineering, procurement, logistics, and installation across surface combatants went to GDIT, not GDMS [S15]; GD's 10-K notes the two units share a customer base and go to market together when appropriate [S3]. The GD family also holds the Navy SeaPort-NxG services vehicle through GDIT [S29].

PP8 — Small Huntsville footprint

GDMS's Huntsville presence is one office at 6000 Technology Drive NW in Cummings Research Park [S26]; public directory data lists an estimated 100 to 249 employees at that location (est.) [S26].

PP9 — Navy submarine services franchise via Progeny Systems

GDMS business Progeny Systems (Manassas, VA) holds a $121M Navy submarine software development services contract [S21], a $66M DARTS award [S20], a $15.3M security-toolkit shipset award that grows to $91M with options through August 2030 [S23], and an $18.2M AUKUS submarine weapon launch and weapon simulation award (October 2025) [S22].

PP10 — DCGS-A loss and denied GAO protest

GDMS protested the Army's March 2018 DCGS-A Capability Drop 1 awards to Raytheon and Palantir after its proposal was rejected; GAO denied the protest (B-416181) [S16].

PP11 — Leadership continuity and recent moves

Chris Brady has been GDMS president since January 1, 2019 and received a sixth Wash100 award in 2026 [S17]. Recent appointments: Chris Jaeger, VP cross business and strategic initiatives, effective January 5, 2026 (three decades at CIA, State, and Army) [S18]; Ron Moore promoted to VP IT and CIO, January 2026; Brendan Grant named VP of space systems in October 2025 [S19].

PP12 — Strategic deterrence heritage

GDMS cites a 70-year heritage in strategic ballistic-missile guidance, weapon command and control, and communications for USAF ICBM and Navy SLBM systems [S27].
