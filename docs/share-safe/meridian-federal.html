Overview

Meridian Federal Solutions (EXAMPLE — fictional company) is a large test & evaluation and digital engineering services firm with a deep footprint at Air Force test organizations and growing NAVAIR presence. [S1] They grew through two acquisitions since 2023 and now pursue full-spectrum T&E support: instrumentation, telemetry, data analytics, and range operations. Their strategy is to prime large consolidated support contracts and defend incumbency aggressively with customer-embedded staff. We collide with them most directly on AFTC range and telemetry recompetes and on OASIS+ task orders. [S2]

Threat Summary

Our most frequent Tier 1 opponent at AFTC. They win on incumbency, customer intimacy, and scale; they lose when evaluations reward technical innovation, key-personnel stability, or price realism. Expect them to prime, to protest close losses, and to recruit our staff during transitions.

Their Discriminators

  • Twenty-year continuous presence at the primary AFTC ranges; evaluators know their program managers by name. [S1]
  • Self-performed instrumentation depot with rapid calibration turnaround. [S2]
  • Scale: can surge 100+ cleared staff from adjacent contracts. [S2]

Strengths

  • Entrenched incumbent on two of the three range support contracts we target. [S2]
  • Strong past performance narrative: CPARS-quality references across a decade of range O&M work. [S2]
  • Vehicle position: OASIS+ Unrestricted and GSA MAS holder; can reach most of our target work without teaming. [S1]

Weaknesses

W1 — Thin bench for certified instrumentation engineers

Customer feedback during the 2025 telemetry recompete debrief noted sustained vacancies in certified instrumentation roles; they backfilled with subcontractor staff at premium rates. [S3]

W2 — Premium pricing on recompetes

Their evaluated price came in well above the incumbent baseline on the 2025 telemetry recompete, driven by out-year escalation the evaluators flagged as weakly supported. [S4]

W3 — Leadership churn after the 2024 acquisition

Three senior program directors departed within a year of the Meridian Holdings acquisition, including both named key personnel on their largest range contract. [S5]
