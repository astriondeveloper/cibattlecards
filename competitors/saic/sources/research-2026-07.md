This is the modern SAIC (NASDAQ: SAIC, CIK 1571123), the "New SAIC" that spun
off from the old SAIC on 2013-09-27 when the parent renamed itself Leidos
Holdings. It is a different company from Leidos (which holds a separate card).

## Company baseline

- Legal name: Science Applications International Corporation. HQ: Reston, VA.
  Fortune 500 technology integrator; two reportable segments (Defense and
  Intelligence; Civilian). Markets: defense, space, civilian, intelligence.
- FY ends late January. FY2025 = year ended 2025-01-31; FY2026 = ended
  2026-01-30; FY2027 = current (ends ~Jan 2027).
- FY2025: revenue $7.48B (3.1% organic growth); net income $362M; adjusted
  EBITDA $710M (9.5%); diluted EPS $7.17; adjusted diluted EPS $9.13; free cash
  flow $499M. ~24,000 employees (incl. 6,000+ active military/veterans) per the
  FY2025 10-K.
- FY2026: revenue $7.26B (down ~3%); net income $358M; adjusted EBITDA $708M
  (9.7%); diluted EPS $7.70; operating cash flow $609M; FCF $577M. Q4 revenue
  $1.75B. FY2027 initial guidance ~$7.0-7.2B (organic decline ~2-4%), cut for
  "procurement delays and unfavorable customer award decisions," with higher
  margins.
- FY2027 Q1 (quarter ended 2026-05-01, most recent): revenue $1.91B (~2% total,
  0.5% organic adjusted for SilverEdge); net bookings $2.1B (book-to-bill 1.1);
  net income $115M; adjusted EBITDA $222M (11.6%); diluted EPS $2.61 (adjusted
  $3.23); FCF $118M. Raised FY2027 adjusted EBITDA and adjusted EPS guidance.
- Q3 FY2026 (quarter ended 2025-10-31): revenue $1,866M (down 5.6% YoY, ~1%
  government-shutdown headwind); backlog ~$23.8B (funded ~$3.8B); bookings
  ~$2.2B (book-to-bill 1.2); ~$515M of space/intel awards in the quarter; NP
  Propulsion Test Facility (PTF) Torpedoes booking ~$180M.

## Leadership and strategy

- CEO: James (Jim) Reagan, former Leidos CFO. Interim CEO from 2025-10-23;
  permanent CEO effective 2026-02-17.
- Predecessor Toni Townes-Whitley departed 2025-10-23 "without cause" after two
  years (joined from Microsoft Oct 2023); board sought a different direction;
  two-year noncompete.
- CFO/EVP: Prabu Natarajan (his 2026 bio cites ~$7.3B revenue, ~23,000
  employees). Chief Growth Officer: Ravi Dankanikote. EVP AFSI Business Group:
  Vinnie DiFronzo. EVP ANG (Army-Navy) Business Group: Barbara Supplee. EVP
  Civilian Business Group: Srini Attili. EVP/GC: Hilary Hageman.
- Reorg effective 2026-01-31: five business groups consolidated into three:
  Army-Navy (ANG), Air Force / Space & Intelligence (AFSI), Civilian.
- Declared strategic pivot (2026): moving away from commodity enterprise IT
  (~10% of the company) toward "mission IT" and engineering (systems
  engineering and technical assistance, mission planning networks).

## Collision surface with Astrion

- Redstone / Army aviation & missile (core Astrion lane): DEVCOM AvMC S3I
  Directorate awards - $800M M&S systems engineering (2021, 5-yr, GSA OASIS,
  ACC-Redstone) and $3.6B HWIL and M&S development (2021, 8-yr +6mo, Huntsville,
  GSA OASIS). AMCOM EXPRESS BPA holder; AMRDEC Software Engineering Directorate
  SCRS task order ~$757M (2015).
- Air Force digital engineering / prototyping (adjacent to Astrion AFTC/AFMC):
  HOPE 2.0 $928M AF TENCAP RDT&E mission engineering (2025); COBRA $1.4B CJADC2
  digital engineering task order (2025); Cloud One $727M (2019). NOTE: no
  evidence found of SAIC resident presence at AFTC (Eglin/Edwards); their AF
  center of gravity is TENCAP/CJADC2 prototyping and mission integration, not
  range T&E.
- Space (adjacent to Astrion SSC/Space Force): GMASS $574M ground-based radar
  sustainment (SSC, Colorado Springs, to Mar 2030); $390M SE&I task order to
  Space Force; SDA PWSA Tranche 3 Program Integration $55M (2025); EDIS $655M
  space ground systems at Kirtland/Schriever (Engility 2019 -> re-won 2020).
- Navy / NAVSEA (adjacent to Astrion NAVAIR/NAVSEA): propulsion test facility
  O&M/upgrade ~$242M; MK48 Mod 7 torpedo afterbody/fuel tank production $123.7M
  initial, up to $1.1B.
- Recompete churn / protest losses: lost CASTLE-NET ($1.4B USACE IT, to
  Accenture, GAO protest denied 2026-05-19; SAIC was incumbent under RITS);
  excluded from and lost protest over $972M AF modeling-and-simulation (2025;
  award to Northrop later reopened Dec 2025 over COI). Reportedly dropped to
  ~11th among federal contractors after losing a major DoD IT recompete and
  divesting non-core lines.
- Portfolio moves: acquired SilverEdge ($205M, 2025, cyber/AI/IC); divested
  logistics and supply-chain business to ASRC Federal ($356M, 2023).
