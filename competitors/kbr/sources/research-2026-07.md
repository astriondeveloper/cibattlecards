## Company snapshot

- KBR, Inc. Publicly traded (NYSE: KBR). HQ Houston, TX. ~38,000 employees in 29+
  countries. Two segments: Mission Technology Solutions (MTS, formerly Government
  Solutions) and Sustainable Technology Solutions (STS, process technology /
  energy / chemicals). Website https://www.kbr.com
- Contracting subsidiary of note for our lanes: KBR Wyle Services, LLC (Redstone
  Army work; holds OASIS+ / SeaPort-NxG per GovTribe).

## Spin-off of Mission Technology Solutions (maps to [S3], [S4], [S5])

- 2025-01: segment realignment - Government Solutions renamed Mission Technology
  Solutions; Byron Bright (President, Government Solutions U.S.) named COO
  effective May 2025; Jay Ibrahim President of STS. (investors.kbr.com;
  kbr.com; galvnews reprint.)
- 2025-09-24: board approved strategic intent to spin off MTS into a separate,
  publicly traded U.S. company (tax-free intended). MTS ~$5.8B trailing-year
  revenue; spun-off company enters market with >$5B annual revenue and ~20,000
  employees, positioned in national security + space. Remaining "New KBR" =
  process technology / sustainability (85+ process technologies; ammonia/syngas,
  chemicals, clean refining), led by Stuart Bradie (Chair/President/CEO), Shad
  Evans CFO. (globenewswire.com 2025-09-24, 403 on fetch; investors.kbr.com;
  govconwire.com; wilmerhale.com; stocktitan.net, 403 on fetch.)
- 2026-06-25: SpinCo leadership named - Michael LaRouche President & CEO-designate
  (joins 2026-09-24); Nicholas Veasey EVP & CFO-designate (joins 2026-07-01);
  brand/name to be announced ~July 2026; separation targeted ~2027-01-04.
  (globenewswire.com; investors.kbr.com; intelligencecommunitynews.com; Yahoo.)
- Interpretation for the card: for ~18 months (Sept 2025 to ~Jan 2027) the
  government business we compete against is consumed by the separation. This is
  the card's central continuity/focus weakness (W1); post-spin it inverts into a
  more focused ~$5B national-security pure-play, so the dilution/distraction
  argument is time-boxed.

## NASA (maps to [S6], [S8], [S9], [S10])

- IMOC III (Integrated Mission Operations Contract III), 2023, up to $1.9B, 5-yr
  base + two 2-yr options. Plan/train/fly for NASA human spaceflight: mission
  planning, astronaut + Mission Control flight-controller training, real-time
  flight execution, exploration-vehicle design. JSC Flight Operations
  Directorate; work at JSC, MSFC, KSC. Supports ISS, Commercial Crew, Orion,
  SLS, Gateway, HLS, Artemis, LEO commercialization. CURRENT KBR WIN.
  (kbr.com; investors.kbr.com; spacenews.com; govconwire.com.)
- HHPC (Human Health and Performance Contract), Aug 2025, estimated $2.459B
  single-award IDIQ (base+options ~$3.6B), 5-yr PoP from 2025-11-01, options to
  2035, majority at JSC. Astronaut/occupational health + research. CURRENT KBR
  WIN. (kbr.com press release.)
- COSMOS (Contract for Organizing Spaceflight Mission Operations and Systems),
  ~$1.8B single-award IDIQ, 5-yr base + options (through 2034), JSC human-
  spaceflight mission ops (systems engineering, R&D, mission ops, IT, technical
  services; Flight Operations Directorate; Orion, SLS, ISS, Commercial Crew,
  Artemis). Awarded to Ascend Aerospace & Technology JV (with The Aerodyne
  Company and Jacobs Technology). Replaces KBR's Mission Systems Operations
  Contract, held since the 2018 SGT acquisition. Timeline: NASA award ~Sept 2025
  -> KBR protest -> NASA corrective action (Nov 2025) -> re-award to Ascend ->
  KBR second protest (Dec 2025) -> GAO denied KBR's protest (reported March
  2026). KBR MAJOR INCUMBENCY LOSS. (washingtontechnology.com 2026-03/2026-04/
  2025-11, all 403 on fetch; nasa.gov release; gao.gov/products/b-423901.2, 403;
  Bloomberg Law; businesswire.com "Amentum Wins NASA's COSMOS" 2026-06-30.)
  WRINKLE: a BusinessWire headline dated 2026-06-30 says "Amentum Wins NASA's
  COSMOS Contract." Amentum is associated with the winning team (the JV includes
  "Jacobs Technology, Tullahoma" - AEDC operator lineage now under Amentum).
  Load-bearing, well-sourced fact used in the card: KBR lost COSMOS and its two
  protests failed. The internal team structure of the winning JV is not fully
  deconflicted here.
- MSFC $570.3M contract to "develop and execute spaceflight operations" at
  Marshall Space Flight Center, Huntsville. Directly at our MSFC anchor. Dates to
  ~2019 (Stuart Bradie quoted). Cited as heritage; current status unverified.
  (executivegov.com; iconnect007.com.)

## Acquisition history (maps to [S12]-[S16])

- Wyle - $600M, 2016-07-01. T&E of aircraft/advanced systems/networks, systems &
  sustainment engineering, PM/acquisition, life sciences, space-medical ops, IT.
  NASA since Project Gemini. Foundation of KBR's T&E and NASA services. Big legacy
  presence at Edwards AFB, Arnold/AEDC, Redstone, NASA JSC. (investors.kbr.com;
  spacenews.com; kbr.com story; en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wyle_Laboratories.)
- SGT (Stinger Ghaffarian Technologies) - $355M base (+$10M WC), 2018-04-25. NASA
  mission ops, engineering, scientific, IT. Origin of the MSOC work lost via
  COSMOS. (prnewswire.com 300603219; kbr.com; govconwire.com; crunchbase.)
- Centauri - ~$800M cash, announced Aug 2020, closed Oct 2020, FROM ARLINGTON
  CAPITAL PARTNERS. Military space, directed energy, cyber. NOTE for internal
  readers: Arlington Capital also owns Astrion; KBR bought Centauri from the same
  PE firm. Citable KBR fact; the Astrion tie is internal context, not asserted in
  the card. (kbr.com; executivebiz.com; Bloomberg Law; nasdaq.)
- Frazer-Nash - ~£293M (~$400M), Oct 2021, from Babcock International. UK/Australia
  high-end systems engineering, assurance, advisory (defense/energy/critical
  infrastructure). (prnewswire.com 301355065; govconwire.com.)
- LinQuest - $737M net ($745M base), announced 2024-07-16, closed Sept 2024.
  ~1,550 employees; space, air dominance, connected battlespace; AI/ML; Space
  Force, Air Force, intelligence customers. (washingtontechnology.com;
  spacenews.com; breakingdefense.com; govconwire.com.)
