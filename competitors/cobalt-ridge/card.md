## Overview

Cobalt Ridge Technologies (EXAMPLE — fictional company) is an SDVOSB range
operations specialist concentrated at Edwards-area test activities. [S1] They
sub for larger primes on consolidated contracts and prime small-business
set-aside range task orders. Growing fast; hired two former government range
officers into BD roles in 2025. [S2]

## Threat Summary

Dangerous on small-business set-asides at AFTC and as a low-cost sub that
strengthens a big prime's price story. Not yet a credible prime above ~$25M,
but trending that way.

## Their Discriminators

- Genuine range-operations depth: their staff ran these ranges in uniform. [S2]
- SDVOSB status opens set-aside paths we cannot follow. [S1]

## Strengths

- Low cost structure; consistently undercuts on labor rates. [S3]
- Customer-favorite technical staff on the current range O&M subcontract. [S2]

## Weaknesses

### W1 — No corporate infrastructure for large prime work

They have never primed above $25M and lack the program controls, purchasing
system, and DCAA-approved accounting story a large consolidated contract
demands. [S1]

### W2 — Key-person dependence

Their range credibility concentrates in two former range officers; both are
past federal retirement age and BD-focused rather than execution-focused. [S2]

## Our Counter-Positioning

- On full-and-open work: make systems, program controls, and prime-scale past
  performance the evaluation battleground (W1).
- Consider them as a teammate before a competitor does — their SDVOSB status
  and range bench complement us on set-aside-adjacent pursuits.

## Objection Handling

- **They say:** "We're the affordable, agile alternative to the big primes."
  **We say:** "Affordability matters at award and in year four — ask for
  evidence of managing subcontractor mix and retention at scale, because
  rate-card savings vanish with turnover backfill."
