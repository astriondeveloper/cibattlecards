## Overview

Meridian Federal Solutions (EXAMPLE — fictional company) is a large test &
evaluation and digital engineering services firm with a deep footprint at Air
Force test organizations and growing NAVAIR presence. [S1] They grew through
two acquisitions since 2023 and now pursue full-spectrum T&E support:
instrumentation, telemetry, data analytics, and range operations. Their
strategy is to prime large consolidated support contracts and defend
incumbency aggressively with customer-embedded staff. We collide with them
most directly on AFTC range and telemetry recompetes and on OASIS+ task
orders. [S2]

## Threat Summary

Our most frequent Tier 1 opponent at AFTC. They win on incumbency, customer
intimacy, and scale; they lose when evaluations reward technical innovation,
key-personnel stability, or price realism. Expect them to prime, to protest
close losses, and to recruit our staff during transitions.

## Their Discriminators

- Twenty-year continuous presence at the primary AFTC ranges; evaluators know
  their program managers by name. [S1]
- Self-performed instrumentation depot with rapid calibration turnaround. [S2]
- Scale: can surge 100+ cleared staff from adjacent contracts. [S2]

## Strengths

- Entrenched incumbent on two of the three range support contracts we target. [S2]
- Strong past performance narrative: CPARS-quality references across a decade
  of range O&M work. [S2]
- Vehicle position: OASIS+ Unrestricted and GSA MAS holder; can reach most of
  our target work without teaming. [S1]

## Weaknesses

### W1 — Thin bench for certified instrumentation engineers

Customer feedback during the 2025 telemetry recompete debrief noted sustained
vacancies in certified instrumentation roles; they backfilled with
subcontractor staff at premium rates. [S3]

### W2 — Premium pricing on recompetes

Their evaluated price came in well above the incumbent baseline on the 2025
telemetry recompete, driven by out-year escalation the evaluators flagged as
weakly supported. [S4]

### W3 — Leadership churn after the 2024 acquisition

Three senior program directors departed within a year of the Meridian
Holdings acquisition, including both named key personnel on their largest
range contract. [S5]

## Our Counter-Positioning

- Against their incumbency (and W3): position our transition plan around named
  key personnel with signed retention agreements; make leadership stability an
  evaluation issue.
- Against their scale (and W1): lead with our certified-instrumentation bench
  and offer named individuals, not labor categories.
- Against their pricing (W2): anchor cost realism to incumbent actuals and
  make out-year escalation visible to evaluators.

## Objection Handling

- **They say:** "Nobody knows this range like the current team."
  **We say:** "The data says continuity is about people, not logos — we've
  committed the named engineers who will be on the range day one, with
  retention agreements, and our transition plan was rated low-risk on two
  comparable takeovers."
- **They say:** "A transition this size is too risky mid-test-program."
  **We say:** "We've executed three incumbent-displacement transitions of this
  size in the last five years with zero missed test events — here's the
  reference contact for each."
